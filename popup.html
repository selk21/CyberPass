Password Security "Do's and Don'ts"

Do's:
  • Use a combination of upper- and lower-case letters, numbers and symbols.
  • Use a unique password for each account and avoid using the same password for multiple accounts. If one account is compromised, all your accounts are at risk.
  • Change your passwords regularly.
  • Enable two-factor authentication whenever it's possible. Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring a second factor in addition to your password.
    • Dont's:
  • Don't place special characters (!, ?, @, etc...) only at the beginning or the end.
  • Don't use personal information. Avoid using personal information like your name, birthdate, or address in your passwords, as these are easy to guess.
  • Don't use common keyboard patterns like 123456 or abc123.
  • Don't use any common words found in a dictionary. Hackers use automated programs to crack passwords using special programs that scan for any word found in a dictionary.
